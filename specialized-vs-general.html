Specialised vs. General Tracking

Why generic diabetes apps fall short for Type 3c (pancreatogenic) diabetes, Creon / PERT users, and the growing GLP-1 (Ozempic, Mounjaro) community — and how the GlycoTrace + GlucoEnzyme pair fills the gap.

The Gap in General Diabetes Apps

Apps like mySugr, Glucose Buddy, Health2Sync and Figu are polished, world-class tools — but they were built primarily for Type 1 and Type 2. They're excellent at carb counting and insulin logging, and weak in three places where an increasing number of users now need help:

  • Pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (PERT) — Creon, Nutrizym, Pancrex dosing
  • GLP-1 injections — Ozempic, Mounjaro, Wegovy (including the 2026 oral Wegovy)
  • Retrospective CGM auditing — explaining why a spike happened, not just that it did

Why Specialisation Matters

1. PERT / Creon Dosing (GlucoEnzyme)

If you have Type 3c, cystic fibrosis, EPI, or you've had a Whipple or total pancreatectomy, you likely take Creon, Nutrizym or Pancrex. The clinical reference is 2,000–4,000 lipase units per gram of dietary fat (with capped per-kg limits). Generic apps don't model that — so you end up doing maths in your head before every meal. GlucoEnzyme is a dedicated quick-calculator built around your own lipase-per-gram-fat ratio.

2. GLP-1 Tracking (GlycoTrace)

GLP-1 receptor agonists — Ozempic (semaglutide), Mounjaro (tirzepatide), Wegovy, and the newer oral formulations launched in 2026 — fundamentally change your glucose response, gastric emptying, and appetite. GlycoTrace treats GLP-1 injections as a first-class data point alongside insulin, so you can see the response pattern, not just the dose.

3. Retrospective Auditing vs. Real-Time Alarms

Apps like the official FreeStyle Libre app are designed for real-time safety — essential, and not what GlycoTrace is trying to replace. GlycoTrace is built for the "day after": reconciling live sensor data against backfilled truth-data to identify why a specific meal spiked. Was it fat content, enzyme timing, phantom sensor drift, or GLP-1 delaying gastric emptying?

4. Metabolic Momentum ($G_v$)

Type 3c glucose is volatile. Momentum Analysis distinguishes between an exogenous spike (food) and endogenous momentum (liver or exercise). This forensic detail is missing in general trackers.

Verdict: Which should you use?

If you have straightforward Type 1 or Type 2 and only need carb/insulin logging, mySugr, Glucose Buddy or Health2Sync are polished options.

If you're managing Type 3c, using GLP-1s (Ozempic / Mounjaro), or want to retrospectively audit CGM data, use GlycoTrace.

If you specifically need a fast Creon / PERT dose calculator — on its own or alongside GlycoTrace — get GlucoEnzyme.
