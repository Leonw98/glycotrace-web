Specialized vs. General Tracking

Why generic diabetes apps often fall short for the unique requirements of Type 3c (Pancreatogenic) Diabetes.

The Type 3c Challenge

Most diabetes apps on the market, such as mySugr or Figu, are world-class tools designed primarily for Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes. They excel at carbohydrate counting and insulin logging. However, Type 3c diabetes is not just about blood sugar—it's about the loss of the entire pancreas.

General Apps

  • Focus on Carbs and Insulin.
  • Simplified logging for T1/T2.
  • Great for general management.
  • No support for enzyme therapy.

GlycoTrace (Specialized)

  • Integrated Enzyme (PERT) Tracking.
  • Retrospective Auditing of CGM drift.
  • Momentum Analysis ($G_v$).
  • Designed for pancreatic complexity.

Why Specialization Matters

1. The PERT Factor (Enzymes)

If you have Type 3c, you likely take Creon, Nutrizym, or Pancrex. Tracking your enzymes is just as critical as tracking your insulin. Generic apps do not have fields for enzyme units, meaning you have to "hack" the app by putting it in notes where it can't be analyzed. GlycoTrace treats enzymes as a first-class data point.

2. Retrospective Auditing vs. Real-Time Alarms

Apps like the official FreeStyle Libre app are designed for real-time safety. GlycoTrace is built for the "Day After." By auditing your data retrospectively, we help you identify why a specific meal caused a spike—was it the fat content, the enzyme timing, or a "phantom" sensor drift?

3. Metabolic Momentum ($G_v$)

Type 3c glucose can be incredibly volatile. GlycoTrace uses Momentum Analysis to help you distinguish between an exogenous spike (from food) and endogenous momentum (from your liver or exercise). This forensic level of detail is missing in general trackers.

Verdict: Which should you use?

If you have Type 1 or Type 2 and only need to track carbs/insulin, mySugr or Figu are fantastic, polished options. However, if you are managing Type 3c and need to audit the complex interaction between food, enzymes, and metabolic momentum, GlycoTrace provides the specialized toolkit you've been looking for.
