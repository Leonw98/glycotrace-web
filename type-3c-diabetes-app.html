Why general diabetes apps do not fit Type 3c

Apps like mySugr, Glucose Buddy and Health2Sync are excellent, but they were built for Type 1 and Type 2. They assume two levers: carbohydrates and insulin. Type 3c adds a third that they do not model: pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (PERT). When your pancreas is damaged, undigested fat and unpredictable absorption change how food hits your blood sugar, so enzyme dosing and glucose are part of the same story.

If your enzymes live in a separate note and your glucose lives in a generic app, you never see them together. That is the gap GlycoTrace closes. See the full comparison of specialised versus general apps.

Built for every cause of Type 3c

Type 3c diabetes has many routes. GlycoTrace is designed for all of them, including diabetes that follows:

  • Chronic or acute pancreatitis, the most common cause of Type 3c
  • A Whipple procedure or other pancreatic surgery
  • Total or partial pancreatectomy
  • Cystic fibrosis related diabetes
  • Exocrine pancreatic insufficiency (EPI) alongside diabetes

Type 3c diabetes app: common questions

Is there a diabetes app for Type 3c (pancreatogenic) diabetes?

Yes. GlycoTrace is built specifically for Type 3c. Unlike general trackers focused on Type 1 and Type 2, it treats PERT such as Creon as first-class data alongside insulin, glucose and carbohydrates, so you see the full picture in one place.

What is the best app for diabetes after pancreatitis or pancreatectomy?

Diabetes after pancreatitis, a Whipple procedure or a pancreatectomy is Type 3c. It often needs both insulin and enzyme management, which generic apps do not handle. GlycoTrace is designed for this group, logging insulin, glucose, food and PERT doses together.

Can GlycoTrace track Creon and PERT enzyme doses?

Yes. It records Creon, Nutrizym and other PERT brands as a dedicated data type, so you can review enzyme intake next to your meals and glucose instead of keeping it in a separate note.

Does GlycoTrace work with FreeStyle Libre?

Yes. GlycoTrace can import glucose from LibreLink so you can review readings retrospectively and understand why a spike happened.
